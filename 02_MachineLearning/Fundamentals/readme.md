Yes, prediction is a massive part of it, but it does much more. Machine learning is typically divided into three main tasks:

# Prediction & Forecasting (Supervised Learning): The computer uses historical data with known answers to guess future outcomes.
Real-world example: Knowing past house features (size, location) and their final sale prices allows the system to predict the price of a new house.

# Grouping & Finding Patterns (Unsupervised Learning): The computer looks at data that has no labels and organizes it into groups.
Real-world example: An online store groups its buyers into "bargain shoppers" or "luxury shoppers" based on their habits to improve targeted ads.

# Generating New Content (Generative AI): The computer learns rules from existing content (text, images, audio) to create entirely new, original things.
Real-world example: AI writing an email or drawing a picture based on your text prompt.
